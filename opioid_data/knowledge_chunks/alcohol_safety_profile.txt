ALCOHOL SAFETY PROFILE

Regulatory Status:
Ethanol is a legal substance in most jurisdictions with age-based restrictions (21+ in the US). Unlike prescription drugs, no FDA-approved labeling exists for recreational alcohol. Safety warnings come from the Surgeon General, NIAAA, and product-specific label requirements.

Pregnancy Warning — Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorders (FASD):
  - No safe level of alcohol consumption during pregnancy has been established (Surgeon General, ACOG)
  - Fetal Alcohol Syndrome (FAS): most severe form — facial dysmorphology, growth restriction, CNS damage, intellectual disability
  - FASD prevalence: estimated 1-5% of all births in the US
  - Alcohol is the leading preventable cause of intellectual disability
  - First trimester exposure carries highest risk for structural defects; third trimester for brain growth

Driving Impairment:
  - BAC 0.05%: measurable impairment in divided attention and reaction time
  - BAC 0.08%: legal per se limit in all US states; significant impairment in steering, braking, lane tracking
  - BAC 0.10%: 6-12x increased crash risk relative to sober driving
  - BAC 0.15%: 25x increased crash risk; visual and auditory processing severely impaired

Occupational Safety:
  - Second most common substance detected in workplace drug testing (after cannabis)
  - DOT regulations require BAC <0.02% for safety-sensitive positions
  - Post-accident testing is standard in transportation, construction, healthcare

Labeling Requirements:
  - Surgeon General's Warning required on all alcoholic beverage containers since 1989
  - Caloric content labeling not currently required (under review)
  - Standard drink definition: 14 g pure alcohol (12 oz beer at 5%, 5 oz wine at 12%, 1.5 oz spirits at 40%)

Vulnerable Populations:
  - Adolescents: developing prefrontal cortex increases impulsivity; early drinking onset (before 15) associated with 4x increased AUD risk
  - Elderly: decreased hepatic metabolism, increased drug interactions, fall risk, cognitive impairment
  - Hepatic/renal impairment: reduced clearance, increased sensitivity
  - Co-occurring mental health disorders: 40% of individuals with AUD have a comorbid psychiatric disorder
