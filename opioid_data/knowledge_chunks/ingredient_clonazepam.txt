PHARMACOLOGY: CLONAZEPAM

Identifiers: RxCUI=2598, ChEMBL=CHEMBL452, PubChem CID=2802
Molecular: C15H10ClN3O3, MW=315.71
Brand Name: Klonopin
DEA Schedule: IV

Clonazepam is a 7-nitrobenzodiazepine with prominent anticonvulsant properties in addition to standard benzodiazepine effects. It is the second most commonly prescribed benzodiazepine in the US.

Receptor Binding:
  - GABA-A positive allosteric modulator (benzodiazepine binding site)
  - High affinity across alpha subtypes
  - Enhanced anticonvulsant activity attributed to strong alpha-1 and alpha-2 subunit binding
  - Serotonergic effects reported (may contribute to its use in panic disorder)

Potency: high (0.5 mg clonazepam ≈ 10 mg diazepam)
Onset: intermediate (30-60 minutes oral)
Half-life: 18-50 hours (long-acting)
Duration: 6-12 hours for anxiolytic effect (despite long half-life, subjective effects wane earlier)

Metabolism:
  - Primary: CYP3A4 nitroreduction to 7-aminoclonazepam (inactive metabolite)
  - Also: CYP3A4 hydroxylation
  - 7-aminoclonazepam is the primary urinary metabolite detected in drug screening

Clinical Indications:
  - FDA-approved: seizure disorders (Lennox-Gastaut, akinetic seizures, absence seizures), panic disorder
  - Off-label: anxiety disorders, REM sleep behavior disorder, akathisia

Abuse Profile:
  - Lower abuse potential than alprazolam (slower onset, longer duration)
  - Street names: "k-pins", "k-pins", "pins"
  - Less commonly found as counterfeit/pressed tablets compared to alprazolam
  - Still carries dependence risk with chronic use

Withdrawal Considerations:
  Long half-life makes withdrawal onset delayed (3-5 days after last dose) and generally smoother than short-acting agents, but protracted withdrawal symptoms may last longer. Direct taper is feasible without conversion to diazepam in many cases.
