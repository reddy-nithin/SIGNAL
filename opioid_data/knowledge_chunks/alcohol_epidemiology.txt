ALCOHOL USE EPIDEMIOLOGY

Prevalence (United States, NIAAA/SAMHSA):
  - 84.0% of adults (18+) report lifetime alcohol use
  - 69.5% report past-year alcohol use
  - 54.9% report past-month alcohol use
  - 25.8% report past-month binge drinking (5+ drinks on an occasion for men, 4+ for women)
  - 6.3% report past-month heavy drinking (binge drinking on 5+ days per month)
  - 28.6 million adults (10.6%) met criteria for alcohol use disorder (AUD) in 2023

Mortality:
  - Approximately 178,000 alcohol-related deaths per year in the US (2020-2021 average, CDC)
  - Third leading preventable cause of death (after tobacco and poor diet/physical inactivity)
  - Alcohol-related liver disease deaths: approximately 56,000/year
  - Alcohol-impaired driving fatalities: approximately 13,000/year
  - Alcohol-related overdose deaths (including poly-substance): rising trend since 2019

Demographics:
  - Age: highest binge drinking rates in 18-34 age group
  - Sex: men have higher rates of AUD (12.1% vs 9.1%), but the gender gap is narrowing
  - Race/ethnicity: highest AUD prevalence among American Indian/Alaska Native populations
  - Socioeconomic: U-shaped relationship — both low and high income groups show elevated risk patterns

Comparison to Opioid Epidemic:
Alcohol kills approximately 2-3x more Americans annually than opioids (~178,000 vs ~80,000). However, the opioid crisis receives disproportionately more policy attention due to its rapid acceleration and pharmaceutical industry liability. Many opioid overdose deaths involve concurrent alcohol use, making these epidemics deeply intertwined.

Global Burden (WHO):
  - 3 million deaths annually worldwide attributable to alcohol (5.3% of all deaths)
  - 5.1% of global burden of disease and injury (DALYs) attributable to alcohol
  - Leading risk factor for death in males aged 15-49 globally
