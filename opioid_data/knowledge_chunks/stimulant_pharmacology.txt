PHARMACOLOGY: STIMULANTS (CLASS OVERVIEW)

Drug Class: CNS stimulant, sympathomimetic
Subclasses: amphetamines, cathinones, tropane alkaloids (cocaine), entactogens (MDMA)

Primary Mechanism — Monoamine Enhancement:
All stimulants increase synaptic concentrations of dopamine (DA), norepinephrine (NE), and/or serotonin (5-HT), but through different mechanisms:

1. Releasing agents (amphetamines, methamphetamine, MDMA):
   - Enter presynaptic terminal via monoamine transporters (DAT, NET, SERT)
   - Reverse transporter function, causing efflux of monoamines into synapse
   - Displace vesicular monoamine stores (via VMAT2 interaction)
   - Result: massive increase in synaptic DA/NE/5-HT independent of neuronal firing

2. Reuptake inhibitors (cocaine, methylphenidate):
   - Block DAT, NET, and/or SERT, preventing monoamine clearance from synapse
   - Do NOT cause transporter reversal or vesicular release
   - Result: amplification of normal neuronal signaling

Dopaminergic Reward Circuit:
Stimulant reinforcement is primarily mediated by dopamine release in the nucleus accumbens (mesolimbic pathway). The magnitude and speed of dopamine increase determines abuse potential:
  - Smoked/IV methamphetamine: massive, rapid DA surge → highest addiction potential
  - Oral therapeutic amphetamine (Adderall): gradual DA increase → lower addiction potential at prescribed doses
  - Cocaine: rapid but short-lived DA reuptake blockade → compulsive redosing pattern

Noradrenergic Effects (peripheral sympathomimetic):
All stimulants activate the sympathetic nervous system via NE:
  - Tachycardia, hypertension, vasoconstriction
  - Hyperthermia, diaphoresis
  - Mydriasis (pupil dilation)
  - Appetite suppression
  These cardiovascular effects are the primary source of acute stimulant toxicity.

Neurotoxicity:
Methamphetamine and MDMA cause dose-dependent neurotoxicity:
  - Methamphetamine: dopaminergic terminal degeneration, reactive oxygen species, mitochondrial damage
  - MDMA: serotonergic terminal degeneration, oxidative stress
  - Cocaine: generally not directly neurotoxic but causes ischemic damage via vasospasm

No Physical Dependence (in classical sense):
Stimulant withdrawal produces dysphoria, fatigue, hypersomnia, and increased appetite — not the autonomic instability of alcohol/benzodiazepine withdrawal. Stimulant withdrawal is not life-threatening but psychological dependence can be profound.
