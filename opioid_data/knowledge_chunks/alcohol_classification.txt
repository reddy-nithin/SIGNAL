ALCOHOL CLASSIFICATION

Drug Class: CNS depressant, sedative-hypnotic
Chemical Name: ethanol (ethyl alcohol, CH3CH2OH)
DEA Schedule: not scheduled (legal regulated substance)
WHO ATC Code: V03AB16 (ethanol as antidote), N07BB (drugs used in alcohol dependence)

Pharmacological Classification:
  - Primary: GABA-A receptor positive allosteric modulator
  - Secondary: NMDA glutamate receptor antagonist
  - Tertiary: non-selective CNS depressant affecting multiple receptor systems
  - Drug class overlap: shares mechanism with benzodiazepines (GABA-A modulation) and barbiturates (GABA-A modulation), explaining cross-tolerance and dangerous co-ingestion

Legal Status:
  - United States: legal for purchase and consumption at age 21+; regulated by ATF (Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives) and state liquor authorities
  - International: legal in most countries with varying age restrictions (typically 18-21)
  - Religious/cultural restrictions in some jurisdictions

Forms of Consumption:
  - Beer (3-12% ABV)
  - Wine (9-16% ABV)
  - Distilled spirits (20-95% ABV)
  - Malt beverages and flavored alcoholic beverages
  - Non-beverage alcohol (hand sanitizer, mouthwash) — relevant for severe AUD populations

Clinical Distinction — Substance vs Medication:
When alcohol appears in social media text, context determines classification:
  - Recreational use, binge drinking, dependence language → substance of concern
  - "I quit drinking", "sober 6 months" → Recovery stage indicator
  - Medical context (e.g., alcohol-based hand sanitizer) → not relevant to substance detection
