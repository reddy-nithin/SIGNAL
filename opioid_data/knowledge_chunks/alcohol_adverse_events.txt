ALCOHOL ADVERSE EVENT PROFILE

This profile summarizes key adverse events associated with alcohol use, curated from NIAAA, WHO, and clinical literature. Format follows FAERS signal reporting conventions for consistency with opioid adverse event data.

High-Frequency Adverse Events (common at recreational doses):
  - Impaired judgment and coordination (dose-dependent, BAC >0.05%)
  - Nausea and vomiting (protective reflex at moderate-high doses)
  - Hangover syndrome: headache, fatigue, dehydration, cognitive impairment (next-day)
  - Amnesia/blackout: anterograde memory impairment (NMDA antagonism, BAC >0.15%)
  - Disinhibition and risk-taking behavior

Serious Adverse Events (acute):
  - Respiratory depression (BAC >0.30%, potentiated by opioids/benzodiazepines)
  - Aspiration pneumonia (vomiting while obtunded)
  - Cardiac arrhythmia: atrial fibrillation ("holiday heart"), prolonged QT
  - Acute pancreatitis
  - Hypoglycemia (inhibition of gluconeogenesis, dangerous in diabetics)
  - Rhabdomyolysis (prolonged immobilization during stupor)

Serious Adverse Events (chronic):
  - Alcoholic liver disease: steatosis → hepatitis → cirrhosis → hepatocellular carcinoma
  - Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome: thiamine deficiency encephalopathy/amnesia
  - Alcoholic cardiomyopathy: dilated cardiomyopathy with systolic dysfunction
  - Peripheral neuropathy: distal symmetric sensorimotor
  - Cerebellar degeneration: progressive gait ataxia
  - Alcohol-related cancers: oral, pharyngeal, esophageal, hepatic, colorectal, breast
  - Immune suppression: increased susceptibility to pneumonia, tuberculosis

Withdrawal-Related Adverse Events:
  - Seizures (generalized tonic-clonic, 24-48 hours post-cessation)
  - Delirium tremens (mortality 15-40% if untreated)
  - Autonomic instability: hypertension, tachycardia, hyperthermia, diaphoresis

Poly-Substance Adverse Events:
  - Alcohol + opioids: synergistic respiratory depression (leading cause of poly-substance overdose death)
  - Alcohol + benzodiazepines: enhanced GABA-A depression, respiratory failure
  - Alcohol + cocaine: cocaethylene formation, enhanced cardiotoxicity
  - Alcohol + acetaminophen: hepatotoxicity from CYP2E1-mediated NAPQI formation
