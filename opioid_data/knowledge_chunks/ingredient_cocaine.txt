PHARMACOLOGY: COCAINE

Identifiers: RxCUI=2670, ChEMBL=CHEMBL370805, PubChem CID=446220
Molecular: C17H21NO4, MW=303.35
DEA Schedule: II (medical use as topical anesthetic; illicit use is Schedule I equivalent)

Cocaine is a tropane alkaloid derived from the coca plant (Erythroxylum coca). It is a potent CNS stimulant and local anesthetic with high abuse potential.

Mechanism:
  - Triple reuptake inhibitor: blocks DAT (dopamine), NET (norepinephrine), and SERT (serotonin)
  - Also blocks voltage-gated sodium channels (local anesthetic effect, cardiac toxicity mechanism)
  - Does NOT reverse transporters or release vesicular stores (unlike amphetamines)
  - Reinforcement driven by rapid DAT blockade in nucleus accumbens

Pharmacokinetics:
  Onset: smoked (crack) — 5-10 seconds; IV — 15-30 seconds; intranasal — 1-5 minutes
  Half-life: 0.7-1.5 hours (very short, driving compulsive redosing)
  Duration: 15-30 minutes (smoked), 30-90 minutes (intranasal)
  Metabolism: plasma cholinesterases (butyrylcholinesterase) rapidly hydrolyze cocaine to benzoylecgonine and ecgonine methyl ester. CYP3A4 minor pathway.

Cardiovascular Toxicity (primary cause of cocaine deaths):
  - Coronary vasospasm → myocardial ischemia/infarction (even in young, healthy individuals)
  - QT prolongation and sodium channel blockade → ventricular arrhythmias, sudden cardiac death
  - Aortic dissection (hypertension + vessel wall damage)
  - Accelerated atherosclerosis with chronic use
  - Cocaine-associated cardiomyopathy

Cocaethylene (Alcohol + Cocaine):
When cocaine and alcohol are co-ingested, hepatic transesterification produces cocaethylene. This metabolite:
  - Has a half-life 3-5x longer than cocaine
  - Retains DAT and NET blockade activity
  - Is more cardiotoxic than cocaine alone
  - Increases risk of sudden cardiac death 18-25x compared to cocaine alone

Street Names: "coke", "blow", "snow", "powder", "yayo"; crack cocaine: "crack", "rock", "base"

Opioid Interaction — "Speedball":
Cocaine + heroin ("speedball") or cocaine + fentanyl is one of the most dangerous poly-drug combinations. Users inject or smoke both simultaneously. The opposing CNS effects (stimulation vs depression) create unpredictable cardiorespiratory dynamics. Fentanyl-contaminated cocaine is an increasing cause of unintentional overdose deaths.
